# xOS — The CEO Operating System

A decision-first, pressure-tested operating layer that sits above all tools, projects, and people.

## Goal

Craig never looks at a task list.
He only sees decisions, slippage, and leverage.

---

## The Core Insight (why PM tools fail for Craig)

Craig doesn't struggle with work.
He struggles with signal extraction.

Traditional PM tools fail because:

- Tasks pile up without consequence
- Lists don't map to outcomes
- There's no concept of pressure
- He's forced to read instead of decide

So xOS eliminates tasks entirely at the CEO layer.

---

## xOS Mental Model (this is key)

Craig only interacts with four primitives:

1. **Promises** – What was committed to
2. **Owners** – Who owns the promise
3. **Proof** – What evidence exists
4. **Risk** – What breaks if it slips

If something doesn't fit into one of those, Craig never sees it.

---

## The xOS Stack (how it sits on top of your 5 codebases)

### Layer 1 — The Unified Commitment Ledger (UCL)

This is the heart of xOS.

Every system (CRM, Portal, Hiring, Marketing, Infra) emits **Commitments**, not tasks.

Each commitment has:

- Outcome (plain English)
- Owner (single throat to choke)
- Deadline
- Proof Type (URL, metric, demo, screenshot)
- Blast Radius (Low / Medium / High)

**Example:**

> "Investor Portal onboarding reduced from 7 steps → 3 steps"
>
> Owner: Jared  
> Proof: Live URL  
> Blast Radius: High

No subtasks. No checklists. No noise.

---

### Layer 2 — The Slippage Engine (why Craig will use this)

Craig never asks:

> "What are people doing?"

He only sees:

> "What slipped, and why?"

**Rules:**

- If a commitment misses deadline → it auto-enters Slippage
- Slippage requires one sentence explanation
- Second slip → escalates to Decision Required

Craig never hunts for problems.
Problems surface themselves.

---

### Layer 3 — The Proof-First Interface

Nothing is "done" without proof.

Craig never reads updates like:

- "Working on it"
- "Almost there"
- "In progress"

He only sees:

- Live link
- Metric moved
- Demo recording
- Before / after delta

If proof isn't attached → it doesn't exist.

---

### Layer 4 — The CEO Decision Queue (the killer feature)

This is where Craig actually operates.

At any moment, Craig has **≤ 7 items** in his queue, max.

Each item answers:

- What decision is needed?
- What happens if I don't decide?
- What are the 2–3 real options?

**Example:**

> **Decision:** Greenlight SMS investor campaign now or wait for portal fix?
>
> Risk of waiting: $X lost momentum  
> Risk of launching: Support load spike
>
> **Options:**  
> A) Launch now with guardrails  
> B) Delay 7 days  
> C) Soft launch to segment
>
> Craig decides. System executes.
